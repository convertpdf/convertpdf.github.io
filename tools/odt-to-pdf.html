What is an ODT File?

ODT (OpenDocument Text) is the standard document format for LibreOffice Writer and Apache OpenOffice. It's an open-source alternative to Microsoft Word's .docx format, widely used in education, government, and open-source environments.

While ODT files are great for editing in compatible software, they can't be opened without LibreOffice, OpenOffice, or Google Docs. Converting to PDF ensures your document displays correctly for every recipient, regardless of what software they use.

Our free ODT to PDF converter handles the conversion entirely in your browser ‚Äî no server uploads, no privacy risks, and no LibreOffice installation required.

When to Convert ODT to PDF

Sharing with Non-LibreOffice Users: Send your document to colleagues, clients, or institutions that may not have LibreOffice installed.

Professional Submission: Convert ODT reports, essays, or applications to PDF for submission to employers, universities, or government agencies.

Archiving Documents: Save long-term records in PDF format, which is more universally supported for archiving than ODT.

Print-Ready Output: Convert ODT documents to PDF to ensure consistent, professional printing on any printer.
