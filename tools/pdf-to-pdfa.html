What is PDF/A?

PDF/A is an ISO-standardized version of the PDF format specifically designed for long-term digital archiving (ISO 19005). Unlike regular PDFs, PDF/A files are entirely self-contained ‚Äî all fonts, color profiles, and resources are embedded within the file, ensuring it can be rendered identically decades into the future without relying on external systems.

PDF/A files prohibit elements that could cause rendering problems over time, such as encrypted content, external content references, audio/video, and certain types of JavaScript. This makes PDF/A the preferred format for legal records, government documents, healthcare records, and academic archives.

Our free PDF to PDF/A converter makes compliance accessible without expensive specialized software. Conversion runs entirely in your browser, keeping your sensitive documents completely private.

When Do You Need PDF/A?

Legal & Court Records: Many courts and legal institutions require documents to be submitted in PDF/A format for long-term archival integrity.

Government Submissions: Government agencies across the EU and globally mandate PDF/A for official document submissions and storage.

Healthcare Records: Medical records and clinical documentation often require PDF/A compliance for regulatory archiving standards.

Academic & Research Archiving: Universities and research institutions use PDF/A for dissertations, publications, and institutional repositories.
