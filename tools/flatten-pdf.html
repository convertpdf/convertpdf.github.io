What Does Flattening a PDF Do?

Flattening a PDF merges all interactive elements ‚Äî form fields, checkboxes, dropdown menus, annotations, and comments ‚Äî into the static content of the page. Once flattened, these elements become a permanent part of the visual layer and can no longer be edited or deleted.

Flattening is essential when you need to finalize a completed PDF form, prevent further edits to annotations, or ensure consistent display across all PDF viewers. Some older or restricted PDF readers struggle to display interactive layers correctly ‚Äî flattening eliminates this compatibility issue.

Our tool processes your PDF entirely in your browser, so form data, signatures, and document contents never leave your device ‚Äî an important consideration for legal, financial, or HR documents.

When Should You Flatten a PDF?

Finalizing Completed Forms: Lock in filled-in form data before sending to prevent recipients from clearing or altering field values.

Archiving Signed Documents: Flatten digital signatures and annotations into the page to create a permanent, tamper-evident record.

Ensuring Print Consistency: Flattened PDFs print exactly as they look on screen ‚Äî interactive layers can sometimes cause printing issues.

Viewer Compatibility: Flatten PDFs to ensure they display correctly in all PDF viewers, including ones that don't support form interactivity.
